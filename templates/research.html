📚Academic Search Engines📚

Google Scholar

A free search engine specifically for academic content. You can find peer-reviewed papers, theses, books, and citations. It's one of the easiest places to start research.

Semantic Scholar

Uses AI to highlight important parts of papers and show highly cited research. Great for quickly understanding complex topics.


🏫Digital Libraries & Journals🏫

JSTOR

A well-known digital library with access to scholarly journals, books, and primary sources. Many schools provide free access.

PubMed

A massive database of medical and life sciences research. Ideal for biology, health, and medicine-related topics.

IEEE Xplore

Focuses on engineering, computer science, and technology research papers.


📖Open Access Resources (Free to Use)📖

Directory of Open Access Journals

A collection of peer-reviewed journals that are completely free to access.

CORE

Aggregates millions of open-access research papers from repositories worldwide.

arXiv

Free access to research papers in physics, math, computer science, and more. (Note: not all papers are peer-reviewed yet.)


📊Data & Statistics Sources📊

U.S. Census Bureau

Provides reliable population, economic, and demographic data.

World Bank

Offers global statistics on economics, health, education, and development.

Kaggle

A platform with downloadable datasets for analysis, especially useful for coding and data science projects. Be careful for it can contain synthetic data.
